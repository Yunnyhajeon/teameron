TIP 78
Severe, prolonged chest pain under the breastbone that extends to the back between the shoulder blades is uncommon but may indicate a rare, life-threatening condition.

This is the classic pain pattern of a dissecting aneurysm of the thoracic aorta. The thoracic aorta, the huge artery where blood flows from the heart, is the main thoroughfare in the distribution of blood to all parts of the body. The aortic wall is made up of three layers and is constantly subjected to pulsating pressure from the heartbeat, especially when your blood pressure is too high. This kind of stress can weaken and tear the inner layer of the aortic wall, allowing blood to work its way into the wall itself. This will cause the layers to separate in a process called dissection. An aneurysm occurs when the weakened wall of the heart balloons out. This constitutes a true medical emergencyâ€”you need to be taken to an emergency room as quickly as possible. If the aneurysm ruptures through the outer wall of the aorta, massive bleeding and death will follow. Note: There are inherited disorders that increase your chance of having an aortic aneurysm. If you have a family member who has been diagnosed with an aortic aneurysm, you need to be evaluated.

Other possible causes of this type of pain include a stomach ulcer that has broken through the stomach wall, pancreatic inflammation, and gallbladder disease. All of these conditions warrant immediate medical evaluation.
